Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a visa to visit Taniti?

Most visitors from major countries do not need a visa for stays up to 90 days. Check with your local Taniti consulate for specific requirements based on your nationality.

Is it safe to drink tap water?

Yes, tap water in Taniti is safe to drink. However, bottled water is widely available if you prefer.

What language is spoken?

English is widely spoken in cities and tourist areas. Many locals are bilingual, speaking both English and the indigenous Tanitian language.

How much should I budget per day?

Budget travelers: $75-100/day | Mid-range: $150-250/day | Luxury: $350+/day. This includes accommodation, food, activities, and transportation.

Is Taniti family-friendly?

Absolutely! Taniti offers many family-friendly activities including calm beaches, cultural tours, and wildlife experiences. Most resorts offer kids' clubs and family packages.
